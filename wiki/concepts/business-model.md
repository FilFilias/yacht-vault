# Business Model

**Summary**: How YachtBay creates value and generates revenue as a P2P marketplace.

---

## Model Type

Peer-to-peer (P2P) marketplace. YachtBay does not own any yachts. It provides the platform infrastructure — listing, search, comparison, booking, and payment — and earns a commission on each transaction.

This is structurally similar to Airbnb (accommodation) or GetMyBoat (boats), applied to the Greek yacht rental market.

## How It Works

1. **Owner** lists their yacht — photos, specs, pricing, availability, crew options
2. **Renter** browses, compares yachts, and instant-books
3. **YachtBay** facilitates the transaction and collects a commission from the owner

## Revenue Model

Commission per booking, charged to the owner.

- **Default rate**: ~13% (exact percentage under analysis)
- **Flexible rates**: Fleet owners or high-volume owners may negotiate lower rates (e.g. 10%)
- **Future option**: A service fee charged to the renter may be added later

See [[commission-model]] and [[decisions/2026-05-01-commission-from-owners]] for full detail.

## Charter Types Supported

- **Day trips** — single-day bookings
- **Multi-day / weekly charters** — consecutive day bookings

## Booking Model

**Instant book** — renters can confirm a booking immediately without waiting for owner approval. This reduces friction and increases conversion.

See [[decisions/2026-05-01-instant-booking]].

## Crew Options

Defined per yacht by the owner. Three configurations supported:

- **Bareboat** — renter operates the yacht (may require sailing licence verification)
- **Skippered** — professional skipper included
- **Fully crewed** — skipper + additional crew (e.g. cook, deckhand)

See [[crew-options]].
